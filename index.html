Hybrid VAE-WGAN-GP
Data Augmentation for Improved Heart Disease Prediction

"We propose a hybrid Variational Autoencoder (VAE) for robust latent space understanding incorporated with a Wasserstein GAN with Gradient Penalty (WGAN-GP) to generate high-fidelity clinical synthetic datapoints, addressing class imbalance and data scarcity in various Heart Disease Datasets."

Problem Statement

Data Scarcity & Imbalance: Coronary Heart Disease (CHD) is a global burden, yet high-quality datasets for diagnosis are often scarce or imbalanced, especially in low-to-middle income countries.

Model Collapse in VAEs: Traditional generative methods like Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) often fail to capture feature relationships effectively, suffering from "mode collapse" (generating limited varieties of samples) and "variance collapse" (samples tightly clustered around the mean).

Clinical Impact: These limitations lead to poor generalizability in predictive models, hindering early diagnosis and risk detection.

Literature Review

  • 01 Yilmaz et al. [19]: Used RF, LR, and SVM. RF achieved highest accuracy (92.9%), but limited by data size.
  • 02 Tazwar et al. [12]: Identified "posterior collapse" in VAEs for tabular data. Proposed Tab-VAE but still had low F1-scores on imbalanced data.
  • 03 Xu et al. [17]: Proposed CTGAN. Outperformed traditional methods but requires further stability improvements for medical data.

Research Gap & Innovation

"While GANs and VAEs exist individually, a hybrid approach specifically targeting the stability of WGAN-GP with the latent structure of VAE for Tabular Medical Data is underexplored. Our innovation lies in using the VAE decoder as the foundation for the WGAN generator to enforce robust feature correlation."

Algorithm: VAE-WGAN-GP Pipeline

Phase 1: VAE Foundational Training
Input: Real Training Data (X_train)
Preprocess: P.fit_transform(X_train)
Initialize VAE (Encoder E, Decoder D)
Train VAE by minimizing L_VAE (ELBO)
Output: Pre-trained Decoder (D_trained)
Phase 2: WGAN-GP Refinement
Initialize Generator G using D_trained as core
Initialize Critic C
Loop (Training Iterations):
Sample real data {x} and noise {z}
Compute Critic Loss L_C (Wasserstein + Gradient Penalty)
Update Critic parameters
Train Generator (minimize -Critic output)
Output: Final Generator (G_final)
Phase 3: Final Data Synthesis
Generate Noise {z0, z1} for classes 0 and 1
Generate Raw Synthetic Data: G_final(z, y)
Inverse Transform to Original Scale
Result: High-Quality Synthetic Dataset

5.3 Effect of Data Augmentation & Optimization

To understand how the data augmentation contributes to the results and its effect with respect to optimization strategies we analysed 4 situations:

  • (i) Models trained on the real dataset without optimization
  • (ii) Real dataset with GWO optimization
  • (iii) Augmented dataset without optimization
  • (iv) Augmented dataset with GWO

The impact of data augmentation is independent to the improvement in performance. This can be observed in Table 2 where we can see that accuracy for Random Forest improves from 86.24% on the real dataset to 92.28% using augmented data without GWO. This demonstrates that the generated synthetic samples enhance the generalization of decision boundary.

Similarly, this can be noted for ensemble models such as CatBoost and Gradient Boosting indicating an improvement in the feature space and reduction in overfitting. GWO works to improve the models performance by facilitating hyperparameter tuning. The improvements introduced by GWO alone remain comparatively smaller than those achieved through augmentation for several classifiers.

Note: Naïve Bayes exhibits a reduced performance under augmentation due to violation of its assumption that features are independent of each other as discussed in Section 5.1.

Spectral & Spatial Feature Extraction
AI Techniques for Remote Sensing & Agriculture

"The realm of remote sensing is being transformed by multimodal data sources such as hyperspectral imaging. We aim to explore how cutting-edge machine learning and deep learning techniques analyse key features for in-depth evaluation of agricultural chemical properties based on hyperspectral data, and physical properties based on spatial images."

AI Techniques in Remote Sensing

Machine Learning

Optimal for dealing with smaller annotated datasets. Algorithms like SVM, Decision Trees, and Random Forests use handcrafted characteristics.

  • Interpretability: Models like RF can easily rank input features using metrics like Gini Index.
  • Efficiency: Operates efficiently and effectively on smaller datasets.
  • Limitation: Poor performance on high-dimensional raw spectral data due to the curse of dimensionality.

Deep Learning

Models like CNNs, RNNs, and Vision Transformers (ViTs) automatically learn complex patterns straight from raw dimensional data.

  • Feature Learning: Self-learns without manual human feature engineering.
  • Scalability: Highly adept at processing massive volumes of Multi-Modal Fusion RS data.
  • Limitation: High computational costs and acts as a "Black Box" making it less interpretable.

Hybrid Techniques

The optimal tradeoff. Combines the transparency and dimensionality reduction of ML with the representational capability of DL.

  • Early & Mid Fusion: Combining low-level spectral features into two-branch CNNs.
  • Balanced Cost: Well-balanced computational resources. Uses dimensionality reduction (PCA) from ML to moderate training data amounts.
  • Higher Accuracy: Mitigates the curse of dimensionality while retaining robust feature correlation.

Data Handling Pipeline

Figure 3: For Hyperspectral Imaging

For managing high-dimensional imagery across diverse geospatial applications, efficient data handling is critical. Case Study: Precision Agriculture

AI-driven hyperspectral and spatial remote sensing actively transforms farming practices by focusing on key indicators.

Vegetation & Band Selection

Vegetation cover and chlorophyll levels are mapped using indices like NDVI derived from high-resolution hyperspectral bands. Techniques like Genetic Algorithms and PCA assist in selecting the most effective narrowband indices.

Crop Health & Soil Moisture

Combining VNIR-SWIR and LWIR bands accurately estimates soil organic matter and nutrient elements (Nitrogen, Phosphorous). Real-time drone (UAV) imagery processed via CNNs or U-Net can instantly detect disease and pest stress patterns.

Yield Prediction & Hydroponics

Semantic segmentation maps crop patterns, while ML models (RF, GPR) predict yields prior to harvest. AI assessment supports hydroponic optimization and smart irrigation for maximum sustainability.

Conclusion

"This review of advanced machine-learning and deep-learning methods highlights their synergy in Remote Sensing. While traditional methods like SVM and RF excel in interpretability and efficiency, deep learning manages complicated trends. To conquer both limitations, Hybrid Models involving data structuring, PCA, autoencoders, and Hyperspectrograms prove best-suited for precision agriculture and environmental monitoring."
