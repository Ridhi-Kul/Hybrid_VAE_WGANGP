Hybrid VAE-WGAN-GP
Synthetic Data Augmentation for Improved Heart Disease Prediction

"We propose a hybrid Variational Autoencoder (VAE) for robust latent space understanding incorporated with a Wasserstein GAN with Gradient Penalty (WGAN-GP) to generate high-fidelity clinical synthetic datapoints, addressing class imbalance and data scarcity in various Heart Disease Datasets."

Problem Statement

Data Scarcity & Imbalance: Coronary Heart Disease (CHD) is a global burden, yet high-quality datasets for diagnosis are often scarce or imbalanced, especially in low-to-middle income countries.

Model Collapse in VAEs: Traditional generative methods like Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) often fail to capture feature relationships effectively, suffering from "mode collapse" (generating limited varieties of samples) and "variance collapse" (samples tightly clustered around the mean).

Clinical Impact: These limitations lead to poor generalizability in predictive models, hindering early diagnosis and risk detection.

Literature Review

  • 01 Yilmaz et al. [19]: Used RF, LR, and SVM. RF achieved highest accuracy (92.9%), but limited by data size.
  • 02 Tazwar et al. [12]: Identified "posterior collapse" in VAEs for tabular data. Proposed Tab-VAE but still had low F1-scores on imbalanced data.
  • 03 Xu et al. [17]: Proposed CTGAN. Outperformed traditional methods but requires further stability improvements for medical data.

Research Gap & Innovation

"While GANs and VAEs exist individually, a hybrid approach specifically targeting the stability of WGAN-GP with the latent structure of VAE for Tabular Medical Data is underexplored. Our innovation lies in using the VAE decoder as the foundation for the WGAN generator to enforce robust feature correlation."

Algorithm: VAE-WGAN-GP Pipeline

Phase 1: VAE Foundational Training
Input: Real Training Data (X_train)
Preprocess: P.fit_transform(X_train)
Initialize VAE (Encoder E, Decoder D)
Train VAE by minimizing L_VAE (ELBO)
Output: Pre-trained Decoder (D_trained)
Phase 2: WGAN-GP Refinement
Initialize Generator G using D_trained as core
Initialize Critic C
Loop (Training Iterations):
Sample real data {x} and noise {z}
Compute Critic Loss L_C (Wasserstein + Gradient Penalty)
Update Critic parameters
Train Generator (minimize -Critic output)
Output: Final Generator (G_final)
Phase 3: Final Data Synthesis
Generate Noise {z0, z1} for classes 0 and 1
Generate Raw Synthetic Data: G_final(z, y)
Inverse Transform to Original Scale
Result: High-Quality Synthetic Dataset

5.3 Effect of Data Augmentation & Optimization

To understand how the data augmentation contributes to the results and its effect with respect to optimization strategies we analysed 4 situations:

  • (i) Models trained on the real dataset without optimization
  • (ii) Real dataset with GWO optimization
  • (iii) Augmented dataset without optimization
  • (iv) Augmented dataset with GWO

The impact of data augmentation is independent to the improvement in performance. This can be observed in Table 2 where we can see that accuracy for SVM improves from 82.89% on the real dataset to 89.26% using augmented data without GWO. This demonstrates that the generated synthetic samples enhance the generalization of decision boundary.

Similarly, this can be noted for ensemble models such as Random Forest and Gradient Boosting indicating an improvement in the feature space and reduction in overfitting. GWO works to improve the models performance by facilitating hyperparameter tuning. The improvements introduced by GWO alone remain comparatively smaller than those achieved through augmentation for several classifiers.

Note: Naïve Bayes exhibits a reduced performance under augmentation due to violation of its assumption that features are independent of each other as discussed in Section 5.1.

Conclusion

"Our proposed VAE-WGAN-GP architecture successfully addresses data scarcity in medical datasets. By leveraging the VAE's latent space understanding and WGAN-GP's stable training, we generated high-quality synthetic data that significantly improved the robustness and generalizability of ML models for Heart Disease Prediction."
